THE ISSUE

Cloth waste is a major contributor to pollution and climate change. In 2018, around 25 million tons of textile waste was generated globally, with approximately 10 million tons being sent to landfills. This contributes to greenhouse gas emissions and air pollution, leading to respiratory issues and increased risk of health problems.

Environmental Impact

  • Mountains of Waste: A vast pile of discarded clothing in a developing nation. This serves as a stark reminder of the sheer volume of waste generated by fast fashion.

  • Slow Decomposition: The clothes made from synthetic fabrics, like polyester, can take hundreds of years to decompose in landfills, posing a long-term environmental threat. Waterway

  • Pollution: Synthetic fibers from discarded clothing often end up in waterways, harming marine life and polluting ecosystems.

  • Economic Impact: Lost Profits: The fashion industry loses a staggering 500 billion dollars annually due to clothing waste. This highlights the financial consequences of unsustainable production and consumption practices.

  • Call to Action: International Cooperation: There's need for international collaboration to tackle this global issue. This includes holding production and manufacturing companies accountable for the lifecycle of their products, including disposal and recycling.
